# Neural Equalizer System (NES)

**A conceptual neurotechnology framework for mapping and tuning brain communication using frequency-resolved signal analysis and personalized neuromodulation.**

---

## What is NES?

Most brain disorders are described in chemical or structural terms. NES starts from a different assumption: many neurological and psychiatric symptoms may be better understood as **communication-quality failures** inside a distributed signal system.

NES treats the brain as a live network of oscillatory signals and proposes that dysfunction can be evaluated across four measurable dimensions:

| Dimension | Question | Meaning |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Volume** | How loud is the signal? | Relative amplitude and dominance |
| **Frequency** | What key is the region operating in? | Dominant oscillatory band |
| **Timing** | Is the signal early, late, or aligned? | Phase relationship between regions |
| **Coherence** | Is the signal stable and clear? | Consistency and synchrony of communication |

Together these form a **Neural EQ profile** — a personalized map of where communication is weak, mistimed, overly dominant, or noisy.

---

## Core Principle

**Map first. Tune second.**

NES begins as a listener. A mapping-only device has lower risk and higher near-term research plausibility. Only after a personalized baseline is established does the system consider frequency-targeted neuromodulation to reinforce healthier communication patterns.

The goal is not forcing every brain into a generic template. It is discovering the range a person's brain already works in — then using that range to guide intervention.

## Proposed Use Cases

| Domain | Conceptual Signal Concern |
|--------|--------------------------|
| ADHD | Beta under-support, theta intrusion, poor timing |
| Addiction / PAWS | Executive-reward desynchrony |
| OCD | Overcoupled fronto-striatal patterns |
| Depression | Alpha-heavy rumination, maladaptive coherence |
| PTSD / Anxiety | Poor top-down timing, hypervigilant states |
| Stroke Rehabilitation | Weak or delayed motor/sensory routing |

---

## System Architecture

**Hardware (conceptual)**
- Wearable high-density EEG array
- Onboard amplification and artifact reduction
- Optional non-invasive stimulation channels for validated research configurations

**Software**
- Signal acquisition and cleaning
- Band decomposition and spectral analysis
- Source estimation and region-of-interest modeling
- Connectivity and routing inference
- Personalized baseline and target profile generation
- Closed-loop feedback during stimulation sessions
- Session reporting and trend analysis

---

## What NES Is Not

- Not a finished product
- Not a validated medical device
- Not a clinical treatment
- Not a cure

NES is a **research framework and concept architecture** intended for white-paper discussion, academic collaboration, and intellectual property development.

## Intellectual Property

The novelty is the **integrated framework**: frequency-resolved routing maps, four-dimensional Neural EQ analysis, personalized operating-range modeling, and route-aware tuning logic unified into one closed-loop platform.
